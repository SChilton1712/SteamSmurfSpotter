GDPR Compliance

Last Updated: May 5, 2025

Smurf Finder is committed to protecting the personal data and privacy of its users in full compliance with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) (EU) 2016/679. This GDPR Compliance Statement outlines how we handle, store, and process your data when you interact with our platform and servicesâ€”especially those involving Steam account lookups and smurf detection functionality.

1. Purpose of Data Processing

The main function of Smurf Finder is to help players and the community detect potential "smurf" accounts on Steam. A smurf account is typically a low-level or secondary account used by a highly-skilled player to play against less experienced opponents.

To support this functionality, we collect limited data from the Steam Web API (which only provides public information). This includes:

  • Steam ID
  • Publicly available profile information (e.g., playtime, games owned, VAC bans)
  • Recent match data, if public

We do not collect sensitive personal data, private Steam account information, passwords, payment details, or any user-identifiable data beyond what is already made public through Steam.

2. Legal Basis for Processing

Under Article 6 of the GDPR, our legal bases for processing user data include:

  • Consent: When you accept cookies or initiate a search using our smurf detection feature.
  • Legitimate Interest: For maintaining a fair and competitive gaming environment by enabling smurf detection based on publicly available information.
  • Performance of a Contract: When you use our tool to search for smurfs, we process the necessary data to provide you with the results.

3. Data Collection & Minimization

We collect only the data that is necessary to deliver our smurf detection functionality. This may include:

  • IP address (used temporarily for session management and analytics)
  • Steam ID and associated public data
  • Basic browser/device metadata for security auditing (e.g., browser type, operating system)

No personal data is collected unless it is either publicly available or explicitly provided by the user.

4. Data Subject Rights

Under GDPR, you have the following rights:

  • Right to Access: You may request a copy of the data we hold about you.
  • Right to Rectification: You may request correction of any inaccurate data.
  • Right to Erasure: You may ask us to delete any personal data we hold, including Steam-related lookup records associated with your Steam ID.
  • Right to Restrict Processing: You may request limitations on how we process your data.
  • Right to Object: You may object to data processing carried out under legitimate interest.
  • Right to Data Portability: You may request a copy of your data in a commonly used format.
  • Right to Withdraw Consent: If our processing relies on your consent, you may withdraw it at any time.

To exercise any of these rights, email us at SmurfFinderContactUs@gmail.com.

5. Cookies and Consent

Smurf Finder uses cookies to improve user experience and collect anonymized analytics. You will be asked for consent before any non-essential cookies are stored. Cookies are used for:

  • Session management and preferences
  • Traffic analytics (e.g., page views and feature usage)
  • Security (e.g., rate limiting and abuse detection)

You can adjust cookie preferences at any time through your browser or our on-site cookie settings popup.

6. Data Retention Policy

We retain data only for as long as necessary to fulfill its purpose. Lookup history and analytics logs are automatically deleted or anonymized after 30 days unless a legal or technical requirement dictates otherwise. If you request deletion of data associated with your Steam ID, it will be removed promptly from our systems.

7. Security Measures

We implement technical and organizational security measures such as:

  • Encrypted communications via HTTPS
  • Regular server security updates and patches
  • Rate-limiting and anti-bot protections
  • Role-based access controls on administrative systems

8. Data Sharing and Transfers

Smurf Finder does not sell or commercially share personal data. We may share anonymized analytics with trusted service providers (e.g., hosting or analytics platforms) under data processing agreements compliant with GDPR.

If any data is transferred outside the EEA, we ensure legal safeguards such as Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) or rely on hosting providers with adequacy decisions from the EU.

Email: SmurfFinderContactUs@gmail.com
